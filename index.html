Evolution Moment ^.^

Representatives of the family Miacidae were small predators that ran up trees and hunted small prey. Presumably they favored insects and small birds.

A typical representative of this family is Dormaalocyon latouri, which lived about 56-47 million years ago. Its weight did not exceed one kilogram.
This animal is one of the few known to science that can be classified as a common ancestor (or close to a common ancestor) of dogs and cats.
